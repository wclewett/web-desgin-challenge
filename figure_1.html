Latitude vs. Temperature (°F)


Scatterplot of Latitude vs. Temperature, n = 550.

It could be considered common knowledge that temperature on the surface is hotter closer to the Equator, and before this study we assumed as much. What we ended up learning was that temperature and latitude do not a have perfect linear relationship, and in fact, the hottest temperatures were observed closer to Tropic of Cancer (23°N).


Northern and Southern Hemisphere, Linear Regressions

Being that the equator is not a linear property, we thought it would be best to split the data into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres. See the below visualizations for the results of our linear regressions. If we were to do further analysis, it would be valuable to split the data at the Tropic of Cancer to see if that raises the correlations respectively.
